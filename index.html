Aviral Goel

Ph.D. Candidate at Northeastern University

Aviral Goel is a Computer Science Ph.D. student at Northeastern University. He is a member of the Programming Research Laboratory, advised by Professor Jan Vitek. He received his Bachelors degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Netaji Subhas Institute of Technology.

Aviral is interested in applying Programming Language tools and techniques to data-driven evolution of mainstream programming languages. He is leveraging empirical insights from large-scale code bases to retrofit new features to R. He is helping R programmers write bug-free code by migrating the language from lazy-by-default to lazy-on-demand semantics. He is also involved in the development of a type system for R.

Previously, he interned at Oracle Labs, Austria, and worked at National Center for Biological Sciences and Yahoo!.
